SECTION 5: INT8_2x TWO-CYCLE INSTRUCTION (4× multiplier explanation)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The 4× INT8 vs INT16 throughput derives from:

1. **Data Packing (2× multiplier):**
   ─────────────────────────────
   • 16-bit MULT_PAIR processes: A[7:0] × B[7:0] AND A[15:8] × B[15:8]
   • One 16-bit Booth multiply produces 2 INT8 products in parallel
   • So 16 MACs (INT16) per lane row becomes 32 INT8 MACs per lane row

2. **Two-Cycle Instruction with Tag Transparency (2× multiplier):**
   ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Phase 1: Load instruction tag, process rows [0:1]
   • Phase 2: Hold tag (FF enable=0), process rows [2:3] with address offsets
   • 1 instruction → 2 phases → 2× effective utilization
   • In pipelined steady state: 2 phases complete per 2 cycles = 4 MACs per cycle

3. **Combined Effect:**
   ──────────────────
   • (16 INT16 MACs per lane) × 2 (data packing) × 2 (two-phase) = 64 INT8 MACs
   • Per G-Tile: 256 × 4 = 1,024 INT8 MACs/cycle ✓
   • Per cluster: 2,048 × 4 = 8,192 INT8 MACs/cycle ✓
